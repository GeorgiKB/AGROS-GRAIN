Premium European Yellow Flaxseed (Golden Flaxseed)

Yellow flaxseed—also known as golden flaxseed—is a variety of Linum usitatissimum defined by its pale seed coat. It is one of the most sought-after specialty seeds in the global food market. At Agros-98 AD, we are a leading bulk wholesale supplier of European golden flaxseed. We grow and process our crop in the Varna region of Bulgaria under strict FSSC 22000 quality systems.

Why Choose Yellow Over Brown Flaxseed?

From a nutritional standpoint, yellow and brown varieties are nearly identical. Both contain 38–42% oil and deliver strong levels of ALA (Alpha-Linolenic Acid) omega-3 fatty acids and dietary fibre. However, wholesale bulk yellow flaxseed sells at a premium. It stands out for its visual appeal and its slightly milder, more neutral flavour.

Main Applications in the Food Industry

  • Premium Retail Packaging: Consumers in health-food stores prefer the golden colour. It makes products stand out in the wellness market.
  • Bakery & Artisan Bread Inclusion: The light seed coat blends into white and artisan breads. It does not darken the crumb.
  • Cold-Pressed Oil Extraction: Like the brown variety, golden seeds yield a rich, high-value functional oil.
  • Functional Foods & Cereals: Ideal for inclusion in muesli mixes, granola bars, and plant-based protein formulations.

Bulk Supply & Wholesale Packaging

As a direct producer rather than a trading broker, we offer high-volume consistency. Our golden flaxseed is machine-cleaned to a minimum purity of 99.95%. We pack in 1,000 kg big bags for mass-manufacturing lines. We also supply 25 kg food-grade paper bags for retail repackaging facilities.

Product Characteristics & Selection

Nutritionally, both varieties offer nearly identical Omega-3 (ALA) and fibre profiles. Food manufacturers prefer yellow flaxseed for high-end products because of its milder, nuttier flavour and neutral colour. Brown flaxseed can leave dark specks. It can also add a slightly grassy aftertaste. Yellow flax blends cleanly into light-coloured doughs, gluten-free flours, and dairy alternatives.

Canadian flaxseed often reaches oil benchmarks of 44%+. Bulgarian yellow flax typically falls in the 38–42% range. The key advantage for EU buyers is freshness. Transit times within Europe are shorter than transatlantic shipping. As a result, Bulgarian flax arrives with a lower peroxide value and better oxidative stability (shelf life).

Industrial Applications

Yes. In industrial baking, finely milled yellow flaxseed (flax meal) acts as a powerful hydrocolloid. Mix it with water at a 1:3 ratio. It forms a mucilage that mimics the binding and moisture-retention of eggs. Choosing yellow flax over brown keeps the final product light in colour. Products such as vegan brioche or sugar cookies stay golden, with no dark specks.

Our primary focus is food-grade seeds for direct consumption and baking. Our yellow flax also serves cold-pressed oil extraction regularly. We keep moisture below 8%. This level is critical for preventing rancidity during crushing and for producing a clear, high-quality golden oil.

Quality, Safety & Logistics

Our Food-Grade yellow flax goes through advanced colour sorting and multi-stage cleaning to reach 99.95% purity. We pack it in virgin-resin, food-safe Big Bags or paper sacks, in facilities following HACCP/ISO 22000 protocols. Feed-Grade flax typically reaches 98–99% purity and serves the premium animal nutrition market — for example, equine supplements or Omega-3 enriched poultry feed. Appearance matters less here, but nutritional density remains high.

Every export shipment from Agros-98 AD includes a Certificate of Analysis (COA) detailing purity, moisture, and oil content. For EU clients, we provide the EUR.1 certificate for preferential duty status. Phytosanitary certificates and third-party laboratory reports (SGS/Eurofins) for pesticide residues or heavy metals are available upon request.
